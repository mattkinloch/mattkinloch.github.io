Punishment

This is what we're all here for, right? There's been some people pouting (Jack) and whining (Jack) that the punishment isn't causing an excessive amount of physical pain or ruining someone's entire weekend, so I had to do what I had to do and put my foot down. In this section, I'll lay out the specific terms of the punishment, so there's no confusion and no room to argue when the eventual loser tries to weasel their way out of things, and we don't have to wake up to shirtless weather reports in the dark.

Punishment: Mini Hot Ones Challenge

Terms:

  • Non-losers send questions for the loser to commissioner
  • Comissioner sends questions to the loser anonymously
  • Commissioner sends a bottle of "Da Bomb" hot sauce to loser
  • Loser cooks their desired amount of chicken wings
  • Loser coats 1 (one) wing entirely in Da Bomb hot sauce
  • Loser films themselves eating the entire wing, then answering all questions
  • Once all questions have been answered thoroughly, loser may deal with the heat however they please

The user will be allowed only 1 (one) glass of water and 1 (one) glass of milk until all questions have been answered. The "loser" will be defined as the person with the least amount of wins/last in the standings after all 18 weeks of the season have concluded. If there are two teams with the same record tied for last place, the tie-breaker will be POINTS SCORED.

Refusal to participate in the punishment will result in the public shaming of the loser and their immediate termination from the league. Don't be a soy boy.

You Got "Lucky"

What is "luck"? Is it the product of a little green leprechaun prancing around the end of a rainbow, sprinkling his magical leprechaun dust around the world? Or was it merely invented by salty fourth graders on the playground as an excuse for why they got fucking bodied by Kevin at first base in kickball? Probably somewhere in between. We'll define a "lucky" win as scoring worse than the rest of the league in a week, but still managing to walk away with a big fat "W". An "unlucky" loss mean a team scored better than most of the league that week, but still managed to lose. To calculate this, I'm going to take the average score across the league for each week then compare all of the losing team's scores to it to determine who was the "unluckiest", and vice versa for the "luckiest".
