You Got "Lucky"

What is "luck"? Is it the product of a little green leprechaun prancing around the end of a rainbow, sprinkling his magical leprechaun dust around the world? Or was it merely invented by salty fourth graders on the playground as an excuse for why they got fucking bodied by Kevin at first base in kickball? Probably somewhere in between. We'll define a "lucky" win as scoring worse than the rest of the league in a week, but still managing to walk away with a big fat "W". An "unlucky" loss means a team scored better than most of the league that week, but still managed to lose. To calculate this, I'm going to take the average score across the league for each week then compare all of the losing team's scores to it to determine who was the "unluckiest", and vice versa for the "luckiest".

Our league average score this week was a pathetic 114.64 points.

The luckiest team this week was none other than everyone's favorite little soy boy Earl Thomas' Brother (Elvis Smith) who managed to score a whopping 12 points under the league average and win. Thank god for him that he was playing against a poverty franchise like Wharf Rat Owner (Patrick Auth). Slightly less lucky, but still having a bit of leprechaun magic in his pocket, were the Wuhan Bats (Jack Lynch) who finished just under the league average, and also pulled out a win, and did so with the smallest margin of victory this week (5.35 points).

No one was particularly unlucky this week, in that no one scored above the league average and still managed to lose. If we had to name someone unluckiest, it would have to be Team female playing (Colleen "Lean" McCormack) who finished 6th in scoring (just short of top half) and still walked away with an unnecessarily and quite frankly uncomfortably large L.
